## The $6/mo Question: What's the Catch?

Honestly, the price is real. The performance backs it up too. Users on LowEndTalk have benchmarked their VPSes running apps like Mastodon, Nextcloud, Redis, and Postgres — all through Docker — with CPU steal sitting at zero and uptimes stretching well past 99.99%.

One user described paying less per month than their previous Contabo bill while running double the workloads across two separate VPSes. Another LowEndTalk member tracked an EPYC server in Singapore benchmarking NVMe reads at 3.28 GB/s — that's not budget-tier performance anymore.

Support is another thing people consistently bring up. Average ticket response time is listed at 9 minutes, and based on community reports, that's not marketing fluff — some users noted back-and-forth resolution inside 10 minutes total.

The trade-offs are real though:

- APAC bandwidth allocations are lower than USA/EU (e.g. SSDKVM-1 gets 750 GB in Asia vs 1.5 TB in the US)
- Some Hong Kong nodes are limited to 10 Mbps for China-direct traffic
- The SuperSaving Windows plans require at least quarterly payment — no monthly option
